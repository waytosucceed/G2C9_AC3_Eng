"W" and "Wh" words


Convention/Grammar

"W" Words:
These words start with a soft, whisper-like sound.
Your lips make a round shape, like you're about to blow a bubble.
Examples: water, wolf, wish, wind
Rule: When you see a word starting with just "w", make a gentle wind sound.

"Wh" Words:
These words start with a little puff of air, like blowing out a candle.
They often help us ask questions.
Examples: what, where, why, who, which
Rule: When you see a word starting with "wh", give a little extra breath at the beginning.

Rules for “wh” words:

What: This is how we ask about things we see or hear.
Where: This word helps us find out about places.
Why: This word is used for giving or talking about a reason for something.
Who: This word is used in questions to ask somebody’s name, identity, position, etc.
Which: This word is used for saying exactly what thing or things you are talking about.
